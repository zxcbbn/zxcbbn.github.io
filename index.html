Questions 1-10 are based on the following passage.

This passage is from Lydia Minatoya, The Strangeness of Beauty. ©1999 by Lydia Minatoya. The setting is Japan in 1920. Chie and her daughter Naomi are members of the House of Fuji, a noble family.

阅读流程

  • 背景：1920年日本
  • 文体判断：叙事类
  • 人物背景：Chie 和 Naomi 两个人物；贵族

Akira came directly, breaking all tradition. Was that it? Had he followed form—had he asked his mother to speak to his father to approach a go-between—would Chie have been more receptive?

Akira came directly, breaking all tradition. Was that it? Had he followed form—had he asked his mother to speak to his father to approach a go-between—would Chie have been more receptive?

阅读流程

一开始读到 Akira 这个人，看不出和介绍部分的人物关系，保持疑问。随后并未描述事件，可以判断是一个背景。

要注意的对应关系

  • tradition ≈ form ≈ 不 approach a go-between
    • approach a go-between
      • 属于 breaking tradition
      • 可能会导致 Chie 不 receptive

记叙文要点

  • 人物：出现了第一个人物 Akira。
    • 关于 Akira 的描述 directly 且breaking all tradition（有点莽撞）
  • 事件:
    • 提及事件 approach a go-between
      • 地点：无
      • 时间：无

He came on a winter’s eve. He pounded on the door while a cold rain beat on the shuttered veranda, so at first Chie thought him only the wind. The maid knew better. Chie heard her soft scuttling footsteps, the creak of the door. Then the maid brought a calling card to the drawing room, for Chie.

阅读流程

继续讲 Akira 这个人，描述了时间：winter’s eve，地点：某一个door（说明事件切换了）。引入了 Chie，以及 maid（非重要人物）提到递calling card事件。

要注意的对应关系

  • He = Akira

记叙文要点

  • 人物：Chie，maid
    • Chie 没听出有人，Chie 听出 maid的脚步（比较敏感）
  • 事件:
    • 递calling card
      • 地点：door，drawing room
      • 时间：winter’s eve

Chie was reluctant to go to her guest; perhaps she was feeling too cozy. She and Naomi were reading at a low table set atop a charcoal brazier. A thick quilt spread over the sides of the table so their legs were tucked inside with the heat.
“Who is it at this hour, in this weather?” Chie questioned as she picked the name card off the maid’s lacquer tray.

“Shinoda, Akira. Kobe Dental College,” she read.

Naomi recognized the name. Chie heard a soft intake of air.

“I think you should go,” said Naomi.

阅读流程

谈到 Chie是 reluctant，cozy。Naomi 认识 Akira。

记叙文要点

  • 人物：Chie，Naomi
    • Chie：reluctant，cozy，不认识 Akira
    • Naomi：cozy，认识 Akira
  • 事件:
    • 看calling card
      • 地点：drawing room
      • 时间：winter’s eve（无变化）

Akira was waiting in the entry. He was in his early twenties, slim and serious, wearing the black military-style uniform of a student.As he bowed—his hands hanging straight down, a black cap in one, a yellow oil-paper umbrella in the other—Chie glanced beyond him. In the glistening surface of the courtyard’s rain-drenched paving stones, she saw his reflection like a dark double.

“Madame,” said Akira, “forgive my disruption, but I come with a matter of urgency.”

His voice was soft, refined. He straightened and stole a deferential peek at her face.

In the dim light his eyes shone with sincerity. Chie felt herself starting to like him.

阅读流程

Chie 走到门口。以 Chie 的视角对 Akira 的特征进行了描写。两人进行对话，Akira 表达来的唐突。Chie 对 Akira 的态度发生转变。

记叙文要点

  • 人物：Chie，Akira
    • Chie：从不喜欢 Akira，到开始喜欢
    • Akira：sincerity，deferential，straightened，refined，soft，serious
  • 事件:
    • Chie，Akira 门口对话
      • 地点：entry
      • 时间：winter’s eve（无变化）

“Come inside, get out of this nasty night. Surely your business can wait for a moment or two.”

“I don’t want to trouble you. Normally I would approach you more properly but I’ve received word of a position. I’ve an opportunity to go to America, as dentist for Seattle’s Japanese community.”

“Congratulations,” Chie said with amusement. “That is an opportunity, I’m sure. But how am I involved?”

阅读流程

Chie 邀请 Akira 进屋。Akira 表明来的很唐突，但是马上有一个机会去美国。Chie 表示奇怪，但仍礼貌回应。

记叙文要点

  • 人物：Chie，Akira
    • Chie：“Congratulations” with amusement
    • Akira：没有铺垫，直接表明来意
  • 事件:
    • Chie，Akira 门口对话
      • 地点：entry
      • 时间：winter’s eve（无变化）

Even noting Naomi’s breathless reaction to the name card, Chie had no idea. Akira’s message, delivered like a formal speech, filled her with maternal amusement. You know how children speak so earnestly, so hurriedly, so endearingly about things that have no importance in an adult’s mind? That’s how she viewed him, as a child.

It was how she viewed Naomi. Even though Naomi was eighteen and training endlessly in the arts needed to make a good marriage, Chie had made no effort to find her a husband.

阅读流程

Chie 的内心描写，很疑惑，又感觉好笑（父母看小孩）；又提到她对女儿的看法同样。提到 Naomi 的结婚问题。（新信息）

记叙文要点

  • 人物：Chie，Akira
    • Chie：so earnestly, so hurriedly, so endearingly
    • Akira：没有铺垫，直接表明来意
  • 事件:
    • 无
      • 地点：entry
      • 时间：winter’s eve（无变化）

Akira blushed.

“Depending on your response, I may stay in Japan. I’ve come to ask for Naomi’s hand.”

Suddenly Chie felt the dampness of the night. “Does Naomi know anything of your . . .ambitions?”

“We have an understanding. Please don’t judge my candidacy by the unseemliness of this proposal. I ask directly because the use of a go-between takes much time. Either method comes down to the same thing: a matter of parental approval. If you give your consent, I become Naomi’s yoshi.1 We’ll live in the House of Fuji. Without your consent, I must go to America,to secure a new home for my bride.”

Eager to make his point, he’d been looking her full in the face. Abruptly, his voice turned gentle. “I see I’ve startled you. My humble apologies. I’ll take no more of your evening. My address is on my card. If you don’t wish to contact me,I’ll reapproach you in two weeks’ time. Until then, good night.”

He bowed and left. Taking her ease, with effortless grace, like a cat making off with a fish.

阅读流程

Chie 的疑问得到解决。Akira 表明要与 Naomi 结婚。Chie 情绪发生转变。Akira 说明如果同意则在日本，否则去美国。最后离开

记叙文要点

  • 人物：Chie，Akira
    • Chie：dampness
    • Akira：blush，turned gentle（说明前一部分很急），effortless grace
  • 事件:
    • Chie，Akira 门口对话
      • 地点：entry
      • 时间：winter’s eve（无变化）

“Mother?” Chie heard Naomi’s low voice and turned from the door.“He has asked you?”

The sight of Naomi’s clear eyes, her dark brows gave Chie strength. Maybe his hopes were preposterous.

“Where did you meet such a fellow? Imagine! He thinks he can marry the Fuji heir and take her to America all in the snap of his fingers!”

Chie waited for Naomi’s ripe laughter.

Naomi was silent. She stood a full half minute
looking straight into Chie’s eyes. Finally, she spoke. “I met him at my literary meeting.”

Naomi turned to go back into the house, then stopped.

“Mother.”

“Yes?”

“I mean to have him.”

阅读流程

Akira 走后，Chie 与 Naomi 的对话。Chie 认为女儿应该对此报以 ripe laughter。然而女儿同意 Akira 的想法。

记叙文要点

  • 人物：Chie，Naomi
    • Chie：waited for Naomi’s ripe laughter
    • Akira：low voice，silent，stood a full half minute
  • 事件:
    • Chie，Naomi 屋内
      • 地点：屋内

发表于 2016-09-01   |   分类于 SAT阅读   |  

This passage is adapted from Angus Chen, “The Dawn of Plate Tectonics.” ©2014 by American Association for the Advancement of Science.

介绍部分

介绍部分阅读过程

阅读介绍部分发现文章选自“Tha Dawn of Plate Tectonics”,发现关键词“Dawn”以及“Plate Tectonics”。”dawn”这个词我们背诵的意思是黎明。我们在读文章的时候就先认为是板块构造的黎明，但是需要注意的是“黎明”给人的感觉是两个状态切换的部分，即下图中（前|后）中间的部分。而后发现“for the Advancement of Science.”前面即使没看懂也能判断文章类型

A journey to the Mariana Trench, the deepest crevice on Earth’s surface, reveals the great Pacific tectonic plate descending deep into the planet where it recycles back into mantle rock. This recycling of old tectonic plate, called subduction, drives plate tectonics and is nothing new to scientists, but exactly when the process got started is a hot debate. A new study may put that to rest by unmasking a sequence of 4.4-billion-year-old lavas as the remnants of the first subduction zone on Earth. If correct, the discovery marks the dawn of plate tectonics and thus several geological processes critical to Earth’s environment and perhaps even its life.

第一段

介绍&第一段联系

Dawn、Plate Tectonics

第一段阅读过程

读到”A journey to the Mariana Trench, the deepest crevice on Earth’s surface”这部分没有发现与选文题目相关的部分，保持疑问。接着发现此处出现了介绍部分的一个关键词“Plate Tectonics”并进行描述，引入新的过程“recycles”，但仍然没有发现”dawn“的对应词，依然保持疑问。接下来一句就上句讨论的 “recycles” 进行解释，语义重心在 but 后： recycling 这个过程何时开始。而后谈到了一个 study，引入了”a sequence of 4.4-billion-year-old lavas“的发现，解决了上一句的问题。但是“a study”究竟指什么，并未言明，产生疑问。最后描述了这个发现（其中 If correct：表明该理论正确性未知或者即使确定也经历了被质疑的过程）对“The Dawn of Plate Tectonics”的意义。提到了选题中的“dawn”。

第一段对应关系

In 2008, scientists studying ancient lavas in northern Quebec, known to geologists as the Nuvvuagittuq greenstone belt, saw that they had the same geochemical signature as lavas from modern subduction zones like the Mariana. This meant that they must have mixed with briny fluids squeezed up through subduction cones and only there. The geochemistry of those rocks could be used as a sort of fingerprint to help identify subduction gone lava.

第二段

第一段&第二段联系

a study

第二段阅读过程

上一段中”a study”所指的疑问没有解答，本段一上来提到2008年的一个研究，返现就是指上文的研究。

第二段对应关系

Geologists Tracy Rushmer aid Simon Turner of Macquarie University in Sydney, Australia, decided to take a closer look. They and their colleagues noticed a distinct chemical pattern to the layers in the lava, creating a unique sequence of rocks. The team thought this sequence could be similar to lava sequences made by modem subduction zones like the Mariana Trench. Mark Reagan, a geologist at the University of Iowa who has taken submersibles into the trench five times as deep as 6500 meters, confirmed Rushmer and Turner’s suspicions. “The whole sequence,” Rushmer says, “linked in with what Mark was seeing in the Mariana Trench.” The team says each rock layer in the sequences at the Mariana lavas and the Nuvvuagittuq lavas describes a step in the birth of a subduction zone.

The key is in how rocks and their chemistry change with each successive layer. As the oceanic slab descends, magma begins rising up and erupts on the surface in layers atop one another, creating a rising sequence of igneous rocks. With increasing depth, heat and pressure begin squeezing different elements out of the slab in fluids. Over time, these fluids change the chemical composition of the lavas so that they become rich in rare earth elements like ytterbium, but poor in the element niobium. The first layer in the sequence erupts before the fluids can escape the slab, but the next layer in the sequence gets just enough fluid to make a partial signature. The final layer carries huge amounts of rare earth elements and very little niobium, together making the clarion mark of subduction zone lava.

The team realized not only do both rocks carry the same geochemical signature, but in comparing the Mariana and Nuvvagittuq, they also discovered the racks and the geochemistry of both sequences change in the exact same way. This finding bolstered the theory that the Nuvvuagittuq sequence is an ancient subduction zone. “Seeing the evolving chemical signature,” Turner says, “was much more robust than just saying there is or isn’t niobium.”

Geochemist Julian Pearce of Cardiff University in the United Kingdom still isn’t completely convinced, though. He says the Nuvvuagittuq greenstone belt might just be too old and warped to have a reliable signal from 4.4 billion years ago. “The evidence would be compelling if the rocks were young, undeformed, and fresh,” Pearce says. As they are now, the Nuvvuagitttuq rocks have been modified by intense heat and pressure “which can mask and modify geochemical signals” through contamination from nearby rocks. Furthermore, while Pearce believes a subduction zone is one place these geochemical signatures can be made, “it is not the only location.”

While those are legitimate concerns, geoscientist Norman Sleep of Stanford University in Palo Alto, California, isn’t too bothered. “It’s not fully sorted out yet,” Sleep says, but the Nuvvuagittuq rocks “really seem like modern arclike lavas” found at the Mariana. When it comes to the geochemical signature of subduction, Turner and the members of the team say that heat and pressure don’t alter the geochemical fingerprint much, and Sleep says this is reasonable. Despite all of the difficulties of studying such timeworn racks, he says “the work done in this Paper is very valuable.”

选择性写作示例

发表于 2016-08-31   |   分类于 写作   |  

选择型写作是考试中的常客：你觉得A，B哪个更好啦？你觉得A是合理的么？你支持这个措施么？xxx应该在某方面投入更多么？

Should student consider subjects that has more job opportunities, like science, math, technology. Even though they are interested in other subjects

一切关于选择的写作，都必然要比较双方的优劣（假设只有两种选项的情况)；而一切有意义的比较都必定牵扯到妥协。鱼和熊掌需要选择，鱼和鱼骨头就不需要选择了。双方各有利弊，且差异不悬殊，才谈得到权衡

而所谓的，‘看情况吧，他们都有好处’，的论调则是废话。只有描述清楚，在哪种特殊的情境下，哪一种选择更合适，才是思考、才是有意义的。
